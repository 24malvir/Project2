Venues

Wanderers Stadium, Johannesburg: Known as "The Bullring," it is South Africa's premier cricket venue with a capacity of 34,0001. It has hosted many significant matches, including the 2003 Cricket World Cup final. Newlands Cricket Ground, Cape Town: One of the most picturesque cricket grounds in the world, Newlands has a capacity of 25,000 and offers stunning views of Table Mountain1. Kingsmead, Durban: With a capacity of 25,000, Kingsmead is known for its lively atmosphere and has hosted numerous international matches since 1923. SOME ALL TIME GREAT BOWLERS OF THIS TEAM
Dale Steyn


Dale Steyn is widely regarded as one of the greatest fast bowlers in cricket history. He took 439 wickets in 93 Test matches at an average of 22.95. In ODIs, he claimed 196 wickets in 125 matches at an average of 25.96. Steyn also excelled in T20Is, taking 64 wickets in 47 matches at an average of 18.36. He was named the ICC Test Player of the Year in 2008 after taking 86 wickets in 14 matches. Steyn was part of the South African team that achieved historic series wins in England and Australia



Kagiso Rabada

Kagiso Rabada has taken 327 wickets in 70 Test matches at an average of 22.00. In ODIs, he has claimed 137 wickets in 89 matches at an average of 27.34. Rabada has also taken 54 wickets in 40 T20I matches. His pace, bounce, and swing make him a formidable bowler in all formats.






GREAT BATSMEN AND ALL ROUNDERS

Jacques Kallis

Jacques Kallis is widely regarded as one of the greatest all-rounders in cricket history. He scored 13,289 runs in 166 Test matches at an average of 55.37, including 45 centuries. In ODIs, he amassed 11,579 runs in 328 matches at an average of 44.36, with 17 centuries. Kallis also took 292 Test wickets and 273 ODI wickets. He was named ICC Test Player of the Year in 2005 and 2008. Kallis is the only cricketer to score over 10,000 runs and take over 250 wickets in both Tests and ODIs.








Gary Kirsten

Gary Kirsten, a former South African cricketer, had a distinguished career as an opening batsman. He scored 7,289 runs in 101 Test matches and 6,798 runs in 185 ODIs, with averages of 45.27 and 40.95, respectively. His highest Test score was 275 against England in 1999. Kirsten was the first player to score centuries against all nine Test-playing nations








AB de Villiers

AB de Villiers, known as "Mr. 360," is one of South Africa's greatest cricketers. He scored 8,765 runs in 114 Test matches and 9,577 runs in 228 ODIs, with averages of 50.66 and 53.50, respectively. He holds the record for the fastest ODI century, achieved in just 31 balls. Renowned for his innovative batting
