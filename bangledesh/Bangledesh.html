History

The 1970s
1972: The Bangladesh Cricket Board (BCB) was established, laying the foundation for organized cricket in the country. 1977: Bangladesh became an associate member of the International Cricket Council (ICC), gaining recognition on the international stage

1980s
1986: Bangladesh played its first official One Day International (ODI) against Pakistan in the Asia Cup, marking its entry into international cricket. Domestic cricket leagues began to develop, providing a platform for nurturing local talent and strengthening the cricketing infrastructure

90s and early 21st centruy
1997: Bangladesh won the ICC Trophy, securing a spot in the 1999 Cricket World Cup. This victory was a significant milestone in the country's cricketing history. 1999: Bangladesh participated in its first Cricket World Cup, gaining valuable experience and exposure on the global stage 2000: Bangladesh was granted full membership of the ICC and played its inaugural Test match against India, officially joining the elite group of Test-playing nations. The National Cricket League (NCL) was established to strengthen domestic cricket, providing a competitive environment for players to develop their skills this is where many major cricket players made their debut such as shakib al hasan tamim iqbal and many more

2010s
Bangladesh achieved significant victories, including wins against top cricketing nations in both Tests and ODIs, showcasing their growing prowess in international cricket. The team reached the quarter-finals of the 2015 Cricket World Cup and the semi-finals of the 2017 ICC Champions Trophy, marking their emergence as a competitive force in global tournaments with players like mustafizur rahman joining

present and few years ago
Bangladesh continues to grow as a competitive cricketing nation, with notable performances in international tournaments and series. The team has produced several world-class players and continues to make strides in all formats of the game, reflecting the country's passion and dedication to cricket. with great players like afif hossain and litton das

SOME ALL TIME GREAT BOWLERS OF THIS TEAM
Mashrafe Mortaza


Mashrafe Mortaza, known as the "Narail Express," debuted in 2001 and became Bangladesh's first prominent fast bowler. Despite numerous injuries, he led Bangladesh to significant victories, including the 2007 World Cup win over India. As captain, he guided the team to new heights and retired from ODIs in 2020.



shakib al hasan

Shakib Al Hasan is one of cricket's greatest all-rounders and Bangladesh's finest player. He debuted in 2006 and has consistently topped the ICC all-rounder rankings. Shakib has led Bangladesh to historic victories, including their first overseas Test series win in 2009 and a Test win against Australia in 2017






Taskin ahmed

Taskin Ahmed is a prominent fast bowler for Bangladesh, known for his pace and consistency. He made his ODI debut in 2014, taking 5 wickets against India, and has been a key player since1. Taskin played a crucial role in Bangladesh's historic ODI series win against South Africa in 2022

tamim iqbal

Tamim Iqbal is one of Bangladesh's most accomplished cricketers and a prolific opening batsman. Known for his aggressive batting style, Tamim has played crucial innings, including a memorable century at Lord's in 20102. He has been a key figure in Bangladesh's cricketing success








mushfiqur rahim

Mushfiqur Rahim is a top Bangladeshi cricketer, known for his wicket-keeping and batting. He debuted in 2005 and has scored over 11,000 international runs. Mushfiqur holds the record for the highest individual score by a Bangladeshi in Test cricket and is the first wicketkeeper-batsman to score two double-hundreds.
